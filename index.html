Data Insights

Power lines account for approximately 1% of wildfire ignitions in California, but fires started by power lines tend to burn significantly more land area compared to other ignition sources like lightning or arson.

Many U.S. power distribution systems are over 30 years old with an expected lifespan of 40-50 years, making them increasingly vulnerable to faults and failures under adverse conditions.

A proactive de-energization event in California in 2018 temporarily cut power to 60,000 customers and revealed 18 damaged spans of conductor, among other infrastructure issues, demonstrating the benefits of proactive risk mitigation.

Additional Insights

Wildfire mitigation requires a multi-faceted approach including fault prevention, ignition reduction, and fire response enhancement. Strategies like structural hardening and advanced protection systems are crucial to reduce faults.

Using machine learning for early fire detection and advanced analytics for fault detection can significantly improve situational awareness and rapid response capabilities.

Investments in grid infrastructure, such as burying power lines or using steel poles, though costly, can greatly reduce the likelihood of wildfire ignitions from power system failures.

Mitigation Strategies

Fault Prevention

  • Structural Hardening: Replace wooden poles with steel or fiberglass, or bury power lines underground to reduce fault probability.
  • Asset Inspections: Conduct routine inspections annually, especially in high-threat areas.
  • Advanced Protection Systems: Use high-impedance fault detection and waveform analytics to detect abnormalities before they become severe.

Ignition Prevention

  • Arc Suppression: Reduce arc duration by using sensitive relay protection.
  • Disable Reclosers: Avoid automatic re-energization in high-risk areas to prevent subsequent faults.

Fire Response and Impact Mitigation

  • Situational Awareness: Install high-definition cameras and use machine learning for early fire detection.
  • Emergency Planning: Coordinate with stakeholders for fire suppression and provide backup power to critical facilities during outages.

Future Implications

Burying Power Lines

Highly effective but costly, requiring hundreds of thousands of dollars per mile and a long implementation time. Burying power lines is a significant investment that can greatly reduce the risk of wildfire ignitions from power system failures, but it requires careful consideration of cost and practicality.

Proactive De-energization

Effective in preventing wildfire ignitions but impacts customers heavily by cutting power during high-risk periods. This technique helps avoid fires during adverse weather conditions, but it can be disruptive to residents and businesses, especially during prolonged outages.

Future Innovations

Expanding the use of machine learning for fire detection, researching cost-effective underground cabling, and developing advanced fault detection systems are key areas of future development. These innovations aim to provide faster, more efficient responses to potential fire hazards and minimize the overall risk to communities.
